> The body is free, yet the mind remains chained.

Throughout my childhood and early teenage years there has always been an emphasis on short hair. Not even an emphasis but a requirement. But why? In school they would force us to have short hair. What about short hair is so much better than long or braided hair? I can only think back to colonialism.

During the colonial times shorter hair were considered clean and orderly, longer and braided hairstyles were seen as dirty and rachet. Even though African countries gained their independence, most people grew up with this mentality as passed it onto their children. And it passed through the generations, until someone questions their entire reality.

> "Once an idea has taken hold of the brain it's almost impossible to eradicate. An idea that is fully formed - fully understood - that sticks."
> - Cobb, Inception

Sometimes it feels like the African people in charge of enforcing this rule are more passionate about it than the people that instilled it in them. This 'colonisation of the mind' in seen in more areas than just hair. Natural hair and jewellery are similar example, rings, ear piercings, necklaces are 'evil'.
