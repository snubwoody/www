We used to own things, but we’re slowly moving away from those times. These days more and more things are going digital and require a connection. You need a connection to watch your movies, a connection to listen to your music, a connection to read your books. But what happens when that connection is severed?

Most digital media is actually licensed to you, not sold. This is mentioned explicitly in some terms and services, and that license may be revoked at any time. In December of 2024, Spotify discontinued its Car Thing, which was a device used for playing music in your car. A company just decided that millions of devices that were in use around the world were no longer worth supporting and bricked all of them.

Books, which are one of the most important methods of conveying and passing down information, are also going digital. For good reason, digital books are usually cheaper, easier to carry and for some easier to read. But if all books were digital we would be extremely dependent on devices and it only takes one decision to erase everything. If all historical records were kept digitally instead of physically, that would be a very volatile society to live in.

Digitalized media discourages a very core part of the human experience: sharing. Sharing is caring, how many games have you played that were shared with you? How many of your clothes are hand me downs? How many books were gifted by family members? Companies do not like sharing, it is less money, much less money, a single person could buy a book and share it with their entire family instead of each person buying them individually. Instead they want a more individualistic society, if everyone pays that more money for them. So they make it harder to share, digital media is typically harder to share than physical media which can simply be passed on.

Ownership is power, it is a form of freedom, it gives you the right and ability to completely own, consume, alter or destroy whatever you bought or created. When buying things like art, you don’t necessarily own the art itself but rather your copy, and you can do practically anything with it apart from reselling. When you instead subscribe or buy a license, rights are taken away.
