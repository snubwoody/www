> **Platonic love:** A close relationship between two individuals in which sexual desire is non-existent.

I feel that there's a lack of platonic love in society. The media portrays romantic love to be all that there is 
in life, when friendships are some of the deepest and closest connections you can have.

We have become disgustingly dependent on romantic love, so much so that many go through their whole lives trying to 
fill a void in their heart. Some even resorting to hopping relationships, starting a new one after the previous one 
just ended. We tend to put our entire essence into them, quite dangerously, hanging all of our emotions onto this one 
imperfect individual. No wonder they fail so often.

If anything, platonic love has less restrictions, less neediness. Friends can go ghost for weeks or months and reunite 
like nothing happened, couples can't do that. Platonic love has fewer expectations, it allows growth to occur more 
naturally. Some couples realise after years that is not the person that they got into a relationship with. Platonic 
relationships expect this growth, even encourage it.

In fact, friendships often span across multiple relationships, each time helping the other heal.
